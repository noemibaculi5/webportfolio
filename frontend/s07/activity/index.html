Understanding the clean technology that powers the future of transportation — step by step.

🔋 Powering an Electric Vehicle in 3 Steps

1

Drawing Power from the Battery

The driver presses the accelerator, which sends a signal to the inverter. The inverter then draws direct current (DC) power stored in the large battery pack (usually lithium-ion) located in the vehicle's floor.

2

Converting and Sending Power to the Motor

The inverter converts the battery's DC power into alternating current (AC) power. This AC power is then sent to the electric motor (or motors) attached to the wheels.

3

Creating Motion and Regeneration

The electric motor uses the AC power to create a rotating magnetic field that turns the wheels, propelling the vehicle. When the driver brakes or coasts (decelerates), the motor acts as a generator, recapturing kinetic energy and sending it back to the battery as electricity — a process called regenerative braking.
